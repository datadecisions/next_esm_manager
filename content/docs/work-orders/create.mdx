## Overview

Create Work Order lets you start new work orders or quotes. The form walks through five sections: financial information, customer information, equipment, comments, and additional options.

## Document Type

Choose at the top:

- **Work Order** — Standard service work order
- **Quote** — Quote for customer approval

## Workflow

### 1. Financial Information

**Sale Information**

- **Branch** — Where the sale is recorded
- **Department** — Department within the branch
- **Type of Sale** — Sales code (e.g., repair, rental, PM)

**Expense Information** (optional)

- **Branch / Department / Type of Expense** — For expense allocation

### 2. Customer Information

- **Ship To Customer** — Where work is performed (required)
- **Bill To Customer** — Who is billed (required; often auto-filled from Ship To)
- **Purchase Order (PO) Number** — Optional PO from the customer

### 3. Equipment Information

Select Ship To and Bill To first to enable equipment search. Or check **Or force enable** to search without customers.

- **Equipment** — Search and select equipment (required). Type at least 3 characters to search.
- **Sequence Mode** — For creating multiple work orders: select exactly 2 equipment items (start and end unit numbers) to create a sequence of orders.

The form shows **Open Orders for This Equipment** so you can see existing work before creating a new one.

**Billing Type**

- **Customer Billing** — Standard customer billing
- **Full Maintenance** — Full maintenance contract

### 4. Comments

- **Private Comments** — Internal notes (not visible to customer)
- **Comments** — Visible comments on the work order

### 5. Additional Options

- **Requires Hour Meter** — Check if the job needs hour meter entry
- **Photos** — Upload photos (drag and drop or click to select)
- **Number of Work Orders to Create** — Create multiple orders at once (default: 1)

## After Creating

When you submit, the work order is created and you're taken to the work order detail page. Any photos you selected are uploaded and attached.
